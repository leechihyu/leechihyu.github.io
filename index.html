Bio

I am a Ph.D. student in Government at Harvard. I am broadly interested in political methodology with a special interest in multimodal data analysis (i.e. {text, image, audio, video}-as-data). Substantively, I am interested in social media, governance, and conflicts. Besides, I am fascinated by the endeavor of making computational and statistical methods, as well as social science findings accessible to a broad audience. I hold an MSc in Statistics from the London School of Economics and a bachelor’s degree in Sociology from Nanjing University. Before coming to Harvard, I worked as a research assistant at HKUST. I also attended National Taiwan University.
